NCA Registration & Compliance

5 questions

What is NCA and why is it important?

+

The National Construction Authority (NCA) is Kenya's regulatory body for the construction industry, established under NCA Act No. 41 of 2011. NCA registration ensures contractors meet minimum standards for financial capacity, technical expertise, and professional conduct. Working with NCA-registered contractors protects you from substandard work, ensures compliance with building regulations, and provides recourse in case of disputes.

Is Palak Developers NCA registered?

+

Yes, Palak Developers is fully registered with the National Construction Authority. We maintain valid practicing licenses and meet all NCA requirements including professional indemnity insurance. Our NCA registration category allows us to handle projects of various sizes and types, ensuring we can legally and competently deliver your project.

Do I need NCA registration for my project?

+

Projects valued at over KSh 5 million must be registered with the NCA before construction begins. Projects under KSh 5 million generally don't require NCA registration, but hiring an NCA-registered contractor is still highly recommended. NCA registration ensures:

  • Compliance with the National Building Code 2024
  • Qualified professionals (architects, engineers) are involved
  • Proper structural systems and materials are used
  • Reduced risk of building collapse or defects

For projects over KSh 5 million, we handle the entire registration process as part of our comprehensive project management service.

How much does NCA project registration cost?

+

NCA project registration fees are based on the total contract value:

  • Projects under KSh 5 million: Free (KSh 0)
  • Projects KSh 5 million and above: 0.5% of contract value

For example, a KSh 10 million project would incur KSh 50,000 in NCA registration fees. These fees are a small investment for the protection and quality assurance they provide.

How can I verify a contractor's NCA registration?

+

You can verify NCA registration through:

  • Visit the NCA website (nca.go.ke) and check the contractor registry
  • Request to see the contractor's valid NCA practicing license
  • Verify the NCA classification category matches your project size
  • Confirm professional indemnity insurance (required for NCA 1-3 categories since 2024)

Always insist on seeing original documentation before signing any contracts.

Building Permits & Approvals

5 questions

What permits and approvals do I need for construction in Kenya?

+

Key permits and approvals include:

  • County Building Permit: Approved architectural and structural plans from your county's physical planning department
  • NCA Project Registration: For projects over KSh 5 million
  • NEMA Approval: Environmental impact assessment license from the National Environment Management Authority
  • Specialist Approvals: EPRA license (electrical works), CAK license (telecommunications), WRA (water), KCAA (near airports)

We handle all permit acquisition as part of our project management service, saving you time and ensuring full compliance.

How long does it take to get building permits approved?

+

Permit approval timelines vary based on project complexity and county efficiency:

  • Best case: 2-4 months for complete approvals
  • Average case: 4-6 months
  • Complex projects: 6-12 months if revisions needed

Timelines can extend if plan submissions are incomplete, designs require modifications, or due to bureaucratic delays. We expedite the process by ensuring all submissions are complete and accurate the first time.

What is NEMA approval and when is it required?

+

NEMA (National Environment Management Authority) approval is an environmental clearance license required for most construction projects to assess and mitigate environmental impacts. It's typically required for:

  • Commercial buildings
  • Multi-story residential developments
  • Projects near environmentally sensitive areas
  • Developments that significantly alter land use

The NEMA approval process involves submitting an Environmental Impact Assessment (EIA) or project report. We coordinate with environmental consultants to handle this requirement.

Who handles county building permit applications?

+

We handle all county approvals on your behalf, including:

  • Preparing complete submission packages
  • Coordinating with county physical planning departments
  • Following up on application progress
  • Addressing any queries or required modifications
  • Obtaining final approved stamps

This is included in our comprehensive project management service. You simply provide the land documents and project vision - we handle the bureaucracy.

What happens if building plans are rejected by the county?

+

Plan rejections are usually due to:

  • Non-compliance with zoning regulations
  • Inadequate setbacks or plot coverage
  • Structural concerns
  • Missing documentation

If plans are rejected, our architects will:

  1. Review the rejection reasons in detail
  2. Make necessary design modifications
  3. Address all compliance issues
  4. Resubmit revised plans

We ensure plans meet all requirements before initial submission to minimize rejections and delays.

How long does a typical construction project take?

Residential Projects:

  • 4-5 bedroom maisonette: 9-12 months
  • Luxury custom home: 10-16 months
  • Apartment block (low-rise): 12-18 months

Commercial Projects:

  • Small office building: 8-12 months
  • Large commercial complex: 12-24 months

Renovations:

  • Minor renovations: 2-3 months
  • Major renovations: 4-6 months

These timelines include permit acquisition (2-4 months) and construction. We provide detailed project schedules during the planning phase.

What factors affect construction timelines?

+

Key factors influencing project duration:

  • Weather: Heavy rains require concrete curing time (28 days minimum)
  • Permits: County approval delays can extend timelines by 2-6 months
  • Material availability: Supply chain delays, especially for specialized materials
  • Design complexity: Custom unique designs take longer than standard plans
  • Site conditions: Poor soil requires extensive foundation work
  • Scope changes: Mid-project design changes add 2-4 weeks per change
  • Labor: Skilled worker availability in your region

We plan projects with buffer time for these variables and keep you informed of any impacts.

What happens if there are project delays?

+

  • Immediate communication: We notify you within 24 hours of any potential delay
  • Cause explanation: Clear explanation of delay reasons (weather, permits, materials, etc.)
  • Revised timeline: Updated schedule with new completion date
  • Mitigation plan: Steps we're taking to minimize impact

Our contracts include clear terms regarding:

  • Force majeure events (weather, government delays)
  • Client-caused delays (late payments, design changes)
  • Contractor-caused delays and any applicable penalties/compensations
